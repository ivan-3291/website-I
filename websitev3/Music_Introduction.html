Music Experiences: Introduction


Guide
Ideally this website serves not only as a straight up recommendation of specific songs/topics, more so a map to direct you onto paths of discovery in different branches of topics. These directions/reccomendations are generally more niche and obscure IMO, which is intentional.
You can start at a more comfortable/familiar point! press crlt f to find a specific topic/keyword. Or maybe just swipe and skim through to get an overview of the topics here! Personally I would try something exotic to kickstart the journey.
I am also making these as a form of tribute to what the past has molded me into currently, appreciatively.


How to use: /!\ pretty important basic stuff
To hide/show the navigation bar, click on the red/pink bar on its left side embedded in the side of the browser screen.
Using the inbuilt player on the website, you can play music from a playlist in the background. Or you could just use youtube no problem there.... Customise the player using the guide below!
Use the summary below to quickly check out all the music and navigate to them. Treat this as a map to explore different genres or styles of music. Remember to wear earbuds/headphones (especially noise cancelling ones). Also beware of "music fatigue" when you listen to too much of the same music (speaking from experience)
For the player, Privacy Enhanced Mode for the embedded videos are turned on, which means viewing them won't mess up your Youtube feed, so no need to worry bout that.
On hovering over a list, an arrow should appear on/to the side of a music item. Click on it to view a comment about the video/music. If you want to modify this behaviour to e.g always show on hover, look below:
To change Comments visibility, hover over a music list's title/top. A 3 dot icon should appear at the right side. Click it and customize to your preference!

Get comfy in a cafe/ur room/in class and enjoy!


Tips and ideas:
Tip 1: Try using headphones(with/wo) noise cancelling or turning up the volume to hear the details in the music, especially in songs. Or try their instrumental versions. The experience was quite bizzare for me.
Tip 2: Sick of listening to your newest/regular loop of music? Try searching up some oldies/an old playlist to trigger some insane nostalgia.
Obviously, some of this music is meaningful to me just because my experience with encountering it. So before you listen to any, do check out the game/series associated with it, as that could be the ideal way to experience it.


Player functionality:
Open/Show the player by clicking on the blue bar at the lower left side of the page.

Player will open and contain embedded youtube video/playlist when you press on a music list title or item. Try it out below!

Click and drag the lower right edge of the player to resize the image.
When the player is hovered/clicked, will display some buttons.

The top right button toggles the aspect ratio of the player. For when viewing videos with different aspect ratios. The aspect ratios are 1/1 and 16/9. If it doesn't seem to work try toggling the rigid/loose button, explained at the next section.

The top left button is the rigid/loose toggle. This toggle adjusts how the player is resized.

The bottom buttons are to do with positioning. The bottom left button, below the button with text saying "rige"/"flex" is the rigid positioning button used to modify the positioning of the player, moving it to top, left bottom or the right.

The Button saying "Rige"/"Flex" toggles how positioning works. On Rige mode, the positioning obeys the rigid position button. Flex mode... Try clicking it!(and look at the next section)

On Flex mode, when the bottom right button is clicked, the player enters move mode. You can click and drag on the indicator to move the player around. In this mode, external buttons may not work properly. USe the button to toggle between move and notmove mode.
